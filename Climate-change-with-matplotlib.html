Climate Change and Global Warming:

The term Climate change includes global warming,but refers to the broader range of changes in Earth's climate system that result in new weather patterns that last for at least a few decades, and maybe for millions of years. Global warming refers to the long-term warming of the planet which most scientists agree is largely caused by human activity.

#Global Greenhouse gasses plot. 
data = [['C02 From Industry', 65], ['Methane', 16], ['C02 From Land Use', 11], ['Nitrous Oxide', 6],['F-gases', 2]]
colors_list = ['yellowgreen','gold', 'lightcoral', 'lightskyblue', 'pink']

df = pd.DataFrame(data, columns = ['Greenhouse Gas', 'Porcentage']) 

df['Porcentage'].plot(kind='pie', 
                        figsize=(20, 8),
                        autopct='%1.1f%%', 
                        startangle=90,    
                        shadow=True,       
                        labels=None,         
                        pctdistance=1.19,    
                        colors=colors_list)
                        

plt.title('Global Greenhouse Gas Emissions', y=1.12) 
plt.axis('equal') 
plt.legend(labels=df['Greenhouse Gas'], loc='upper left') 
plt.rc('font', size=20)

plt.show()

#CO2 Global Emissions from 1900 to 2019 plot
FILE = 'global_CO2_emissions.txt'

with open(FILE, 'r') as fdin:
    list_of_data1 = [line.strip() for  line in fdin]

list_of_data2 = [float(data) for data in list_of_data1]
years = range(1900, 2020)

data_dictionary = dict(zip(years, list_of_data2))
x = list(data_dictionary.keys())
y = list(data_dictionary.values())

plt.plot(x, y, "r")
plt.title('CO2 Global Emissions from 1900 to 2019', fontsize=20, pad=30)
plt.xlabel('Years', fontsize=14)
plt.ylabel('Gigatons per year', fontsize=14)
plt.grid(True)
plt.rcParams['figure.figsize'] = [11, 8]

plt.show()

with open(FILE, 'r') as fdin:
     list_of_data1 = [line.strip() for  line in fdin]

list_of_data2 = [float(data) for data in list_of_data1]

y = list(data_dictionary.values())

Individual Solutions to Global Warming:

  • Use energy wisely:
    • Wash clothes in cold or warm (not hot) water.
    • Unplug electronics when youre not using them.
    • Change to energy-efficient light bulbs.

  • Recycle - Reduce - Reuse.

  • Eat for a climate-stable planet:
    • Eat meat-free meals.
    • Don't waste food or water.

  • Spread the word.
